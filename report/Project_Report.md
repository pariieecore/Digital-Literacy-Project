#### Introduction:





###### This project focuses on developing essential digital literacy skills required for students in today’s digital world. It includes practical tasks that help in understanding online tools, communication, and cyber safety.







#### Task-1:





###### I created a digital literacy infographic using Canva, as it is an easy-to-use and beginner-friendly design tool. The infographic focuses on important aspects of digital literacy such as understanding what digital literacy means, using useful digital tools, and following safe internet practices. I also included points about maintaining a professional online presence and proper email etiquette.

###### 

###### The aim of my design was to make the information simple and visually appealing so that students can easily understand it at a glance. I used icons, colors, and short bullet points to make the content more engaging and clear.

###### 

###### While creating the infographic, I found it slightly challenging to organize all the information neatly in a limited space. However, it was interesting to experiment with different layouts and designs. This task helped me understand the importance of presenting information in a clear and visually effective way.







#### Task-2:

#### 



###### I created my digital portfolio by setting up profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. These platforms are important for building a professional online presence and showcasing skills. GitHub is mainly used to store and manage coding projects, while LinkedIn helps in building a professional network and connecting with recruiters and peers. Kaggle is a platform for learning data science and participating in competitions.

###### 

###### While setting up these profiles, I focused on keeping the information clear and professional. I added my educational details and wrote a short description about my interests. This task helped me understand how important it is to maintain a strong digital presence from the beginning of my academic journey.

###### 

###### Over the next four years, I plan to regularly update these profiles by adding my projects, skills, and achievements. This will help me build a strong portfolio for internships and future job opportunities.







#### Task-3:



###### 

###### In this task, I explored both coding practice platforms and collaboration tools. For the coding part, I created an account on HackerRank and completed a beginner-level problem. This helped me understand how coding platforms work and how they can be used to improve problem-solving skills. It also gave me confidence to start practicing programming regularly.

I also viewed the responses in Google Sheets, which helped me understand how data can be collected and analyzed easily.

###### 

###### This task was interesting because it introduced me to practical tools that are useful for both academic and professional purposes. Coding platforms will help me improve my technical skills over time, while tools like Google Forms can be used for surveys, quizzes, and group activities. Overall, this task enhanced my understanding of how digital platforms can be used effectively for learning and collaboration.

#### Task-4:

#### 



###### In today’s digital world, poor communication can easily lead to misunderstandings and missed opportunities. For example, if a student sends an email to a professor without a proper subject line, greeting, or clear message, it may be ignored or misunderstood. Similarly, using informal language or incomplete information in professional emails can create a negative impression.

###### 

###### In this task, I learned the importance of maintaining proper email etiquette, such as using a clear subject line, polite tone, and structured content. Writing professional emails helped me understand how to communicate effectively in academic and professional settings.

###### 

###### I also created a checklist of social media do’s and don’ts, which highlighted the importance of responsible online behavior. Overall, this task improved my awareness of digital communication and how small mistakes can be avoided with proper practices.

#### Task-5:





###### While researching cybercrime, I was surprised by how easily people can become victims of online fraud, especially through phishing and UPI scams. Many attacks look very genuine, making it difficult to identify them at first glance. I realized that even small mistakes, like clicking on a suspicious link or sharing an OTP, can lead to serious consequences such as financial loss or misuse of personal information.

###### 

###### This task made me more aware of the importance of staying alert while using the internet. One important habit I will change is to always verify the source of emails, messages, or links before taking any action. I will also be more careful while making online payments and ensure that I never share sensitive details with anyone.
