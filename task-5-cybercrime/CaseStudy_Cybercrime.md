## **Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack**

## 

###### Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. It is one of the most common online fraud methods today.

###### 

###### A typical phishing attack begins with the victim receiving an email or message that appears to be from a legitimate organization, such as a bank or a popular website. The message often creates a sense of urgency, such as warning that the account will be blocked or compromised. It usually contains a link that directs the user to a fake website that looks very similar to the original one. When the user enters their login credentials or personal information, the attacker captures this data. In some cases, the victim may also be asked to enter an OTP, which is then used to access their bank account.

###### 

###### Phishing attacks commonly target students, working professionals, and elderly individuals who may not always verify the authenticity of such messages. The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

###### 

###### This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting with emails and links online, and always verifying the source before sharing any sensitive information
